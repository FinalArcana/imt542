The project incorporates USDA Foundation Foods and F MAP datasets that provide stable 
identifiers such as FDC IDs and EFPG codes, supporting long term traceability and global reference (F1). 
These identifiers are preserved throughout all merged tables, ensuring that metadata clearly links back 
to the original data sources (F3, I3). Both datasets include rich, domain standard metadata 
such as nutrient definitions, measurement units, and food category taxonomies, which the project retains 
to maintain interpretability and meaningful reuse (F2, R1). Because the data are accessed through openly 
available USDA JSON and CSV formats, they remain retrievable using standardized and universally 
implementable protocols (A1, A1.1). The EFPG category system is a structured and publicly documented 
USDA taxonomy that provides consistent terminology for food categories and supports interoperability, 
even though it is not a community maintained vocabulary in the formal FAIR sense (I1, I2). 
Clear citations and source documentation ensure transparent provenance and appropriate reuse conditions, 
consistent with responsible data management practices (R1.2, R1.1).
